BRIEF
Draw on the literature in the field to create advice for a social design practitioner in the public domain.

DESIGN METHODS
literature review, mind map, interview, practice analysis

ABSTRACT
The article explores the multifaceted role of the designer as a facilitator and mediator in addressing grand societal challenges. It emphasizes how designers can bridge the gap between community needs and governmental decision-makers, advocating for a community-led design approach that empowers local populations while acknowledging the necessity of government engagement for systemic change. It also critically examines the political implications of design, stressing the importance of justice-oriented and non-human-centered perspectives, and discusses the challenges of measuring qualitative impact in social design projects, ultimately envisioning a future where designers embrace humility and a playful aesthetic.

LEARNING OBJECTIVES
The project aimed to bridge the gap between theory and practice in design for societal missions by comparing academic literature with conversations with practitioners. The outcome is an article analysing the work of a previously interviewed professional, exploring how their practice could be integrated with concepts derived from academic readings.

THE KNOWLEDGE FRAMEWORK
The Knowledge Framework is a tool that collects, synthesises and visualises theories and concepts from academic literature on societal missions, making it easy to identify relationships between fields and perspectives. It also served as the basis for semi-structured interviews with experts in the field.
